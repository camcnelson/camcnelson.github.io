Branched Cubes

While playing around with virtual blocks, I found out the following by construction: If you take a cube, and any n of its six faces, and afix identical cubes to those faces, then the union of these cubes is a polyhedron that can be stacked to fill space without gaps or overlaps. While coming up with a name for this kind of shape (I'll call them "branched cubes" for now), I came across this demonstration of the case when n=6, with very nicely rendered animations of the assembly. This result is interesting to me because Wikipedia's page on honeycombs says of non-convex space-filling polyhedra that "documented examples are rare." I think this must just be an oversight but either way, I find these shapes especially fascinating for the satisfying ways they interlock. Each unique kind of branched cube, up to symmetry, is shown here, as well as a 6x6x6 tiling.
